La Isla De El Encanto A Brief History

Since the time people started wearing clothes and simple wear became haute couture, it was only a matter of time that dogs would follow suit. Dog collars were the first items to be used as decoration. The first decorative collar was discovered in the tomb of the ancient Egyptian nobleman, Maiharpiri in 1440 BC. There are also silver, gold, silk, and velvet decorative dog collars from the time of King Henry VIII. Nobility and the upper class have been decorating their canine companions for centuries. There is photographic evidence from 1900 of people dressing their dogs in human costumes. Since then, the canine fashion industry has become a global phenomenon, set to top $50 billion in 2017.
As people use clothes as a means of self expression, it has now extended to their pets. Clothing for dogs ranges from matching outfits with their owners, clothes for all climes, and dressing for all the holidays. It is not unusual to see dogs dressed in formal wear for those special occasions, haute couture for the runway shows, silly costumes for parades, and just to embarass the pet. There is now a National Dress Up Your Pet Day that occurs on January 14 while it may not be a very well-known holiday, it is one more reason to dress up "man's best friend".
